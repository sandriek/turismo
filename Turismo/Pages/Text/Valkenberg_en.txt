To 1812 did this park service as castle garden for the Lords of Breda. The park owes its name to a Falcons House, which stood on the edge of it and from which the Castle residents and their guests the falconry. The park was really a forest with tall trees of origin in the near future. In the seventeenth century disappeared that character by the construction of a garden to French style. One replica of the 17 images is away in the park, in a specially constructed bed. This bed is a reminder of the French style garden. The original image is on display at the Breda's Museum. In 1995 the valkenberg a new look. In the whole park shifted his paths, shrubs and trees cleared away and new lighting. In addition, there are new banks in the park and play facilities for children.
