4. If you pass the bridge, you will see on your left the Nassau-Baronie monument.

At the entrance to the city park, the valkenberg, stands a monument you something that talks about the history of the city of Breda, but mainly about the Lords of the city from the House of Nassau and the 500-year-old link between Breda and the House of Nassau. On 3 July 1905, the Nassau-Baronie monument, as it officially called, with lots of festive show by Queen Wilhelmina revealed. The picture reminds of the fact, that in 1404 Count Engelbert, the first Phoenix Nassau and his consort, Johanna of Polanen, were inaugurated as Lord and Lady of Breda. The designer is the well known Dr. P.J.H. Cuypers, which, among other things, the Rijksmuseum and the Amsterdam Central Station. On this monument are not only the coats of arms of twenty municipalities in and around de Baronie made but also the lion of Nassau who towers above all with King Crown, sword and coat of arms. In the three reliefs is the ' happy incomste ' of count Engelbert and his consort. De poorters offer the key to the city.
