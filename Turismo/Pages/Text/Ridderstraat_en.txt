The Knight Street is a shopping street in the city centre of Breda. The street leads you from the heart to the main market square. On the Grand place you can find many restaurants and cosy terraces. Especially with good weather on the Grand place. The Knight Street is characterized by the copper commemorative tiles in the street. In the early 20th century the Carnival party banned by the Catholic Church. On the question of when the party would return the Inhabitants ' replied again With St. never's day '. In 1936, the party again and decided the retailers from the Knight Street and a tile for Prince Carnival every year. In 1991 the Foundation donated to promote the Carnival celebration St. never's day figurine to the inhabitants of Breda. This figurine can be found on the corner of the Knight Street and market square.
